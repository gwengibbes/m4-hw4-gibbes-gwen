Robert Nesta Marley

About the artist

Robert Nesta Marley, more popularly known as Bob Marley is a reggae artist who was born in Jamaica. He put Jamaica on the map, making it a household name and a bucket list destination for many people. He is known for his music and his spirituality. The positive message that he provides through his music has been inspirational for people from all walks of life. He is an icon not only in the Caribbean but all across the globe.

Early Life

Bob Marley was born in Nine Mile, Jamaica on 6 February, 1945. He was born to a Jamaican mother and an English father. He grew up in Jamaican, living with his mother and infrequently seeing his father. He attended primary school as well as Junior High School in Jamaica. It was in primary school that he started playing musc with a childhood friend. This childhood friend would eventually be one of the original members of "The Wailers," who Bob Marley would form his first musical group with.

Artistic Career

Bob Marley started his music career in his teenage years, with childhood friends Bunny Wailer and Peter Tosh. He recorded his first songs in February, 1962. Two years later in February, 1964 Bob Marely would see his first hit at a Jamaican Number 1.

Bob Marley and his group "The Wailers," spent many years attempting to make it on the big stage. In 1974, they eventually moved to Island Records and this is when the group started their journey on becoming known world-wide. It was in 1974 that the group would his number 1 on the Billboard Hot 100. This was also the year that The Wailers disbanded.

Bob Marely continued recording as "Bob Marely and The Wailers," and continued to do so until his death in 1981.

Personal Life

Bob Marley married Rita Anderson on 10 February, 1966. Together they had four children. Bob Marley also had many other children. He is said to have 11 children in all.

Other than music, football was a passion of Bob Marley. He followed the foootball player Pele and was also a fan of English football club, Tottenham Hotspur.
